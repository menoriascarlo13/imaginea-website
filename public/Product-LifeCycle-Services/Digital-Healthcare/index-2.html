Where is digital healthcare spending money?

An array of health IT solutions is sweeping today's health market. But where exactly is digital healthcare headed to? Where are care providers investing their money? Are consumers willing to use their solutions? And should care providers 'have' to be part of the digital health race, or can they choose to stay away?

Digital healthcare continues to be a hotbed of innovation. The past few years saw a high-tide of $2.9 trillion in healthcare expenditure by U.S. citizens, and by 2017 that number is expected to reach $4.3 trillion. As annual healthcare expenses rise, healthcare experts are mulling over how going digital can reduce delivery costs and at the same time increase market spread.

INVESTMENT FOCUS

In a report by Deloitte, 73% of physicians in the U.S. believe that health IT solutions will improve the quality of healthcare services in the long-term. That optimism has buoyed investments in technology verticals like big data & analytics, population health, health navigation, diagnostics, consumer health, genomics and clinical research from a modest $2.9 billion investment in 2013 to $6.5 billion in 2014.


Investments in US healthcare verticals

A recent report by StartUp Health denotes that about 7,500 start-ups are currently working on building innovative health solutions. Popular technologies like mobility, connected devices and sensor-based medical devices are attracting healthcare providers to join the digital revolution, while physicians continue to adopt more and more Health IT [HIT] solutions in their practice like remote health monitoring and diagnosis services using mobile and web.

USER CONNECT

More statistics juggle in the backdrop, but how effective are these digital health solutions for caregivers and consumers? Reports indicate that 90% of U.S. patients prefer self-managing their healthcare requirements like accessing medical information, refilling prescriptions and booking appointments using the internet, mobile or e-mail while an estimated 19% of smartphone users use mobile apps for health & fitness.

On the other hand, 43% of physicians use mobile technology for clinical purposes while many healthcare providers are currently synchronizing their services towards mobile and web-based platforms.


Popular healthcare IT solutions

COST ADVANTAGE

Going digital helps in cutting-down costs too. A report by Juniper predicts that the highest revenue grossing remote patient-monitoring device is expected to save up to $36 billion for global healthcare systems in the future. EHR (Electronic Health Record) another widely used digital data system is expected to save $78 billion between 2014 and 2019 for the healthcare industry, while mobile health apps are increasingly replacing health consultations.

Clearly, most digital healthcare spend is channeled towards enhancing customer experience, faster connectivity and efficient collaboration that provides sustainable healthcare solutions for everyone, at minimal costs. Considering the current global healthcare scenario, care providers with advanced digital health solutions will easily outrun those without.
