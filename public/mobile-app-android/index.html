Guidelines for Android App Development:

Working with Luvocracy and clients across several other industries, we've developed some guidelines specific to Android app development and are sharing our learnings with you.

The Android Launch Checklist

This is your starting point. Knowing what it takes to get your app published in the Play Store sets you up for early success. It's far easier and highly recommended that you follow Andorid best practices during the development process instead of thinking about it right before the app is ready for publishing.

Optimize across Devices

Having your app ready for both phone and tablet puts you in a favorable position with Google. Right at the onset of development, factor in how your app performs and looks in each environment. It's a best practice to design different layouts for different screen orientations (landscape and portrait) across devices and ensure visual assets are optimized for different resolutions. For example, your screen items may stretch out in a tablet in landscape orientation if not previously optimized.

Support Multiple SDK but Set Your Limit

In Android, supporting across SDKs is one of the biggest challenges for developers. Be prepared for dealing with the challenges of device fragmentation. Our two cents. It's a good idea to decide what range of SDK you can reasonably support. In our experience, 10 (Ginger Bread, 2.3.3 - 19 (KitKat, 4.4.2) is a good number. It's easier to manage when you undertake UI overhauls, not to mention managing the burden of testing across devices.

Memory Optimization

The most significant performance issues with Mobile applications arise from how they interact with the device memory or vice-versa. A minor mismatch and the application will crash; often multiples times in a day, frustrating users. For example, e-retail apps need to display high quality images in large volumes and support long browsing sessions as users take their time to shop. This will impact your app performance unless you preemptively tested it for such scenarios. Memory defects testing and app optimization for performance in a variety of memory-usage scenarios is nearly impossible to achieve manually, so plan for automation.

Leverage Android's Quick Approval Process to Your Advantage

While there are no set rules, in our experience we've found that an app for Google Play can be approved and hit the stores as quickly as 4-6 hours after submission. A word of caution! While this speedy approval allows developers to quickly push updates and changes to the app, beware of pushing out buggy updates. Take the time to test - combine automation and manual testing for each and every submission to the Play store.
