your way to Success

We worked with a leading Configure-Price-Quote (CPQ) provider to meet the highly aggressive demands of their fast-paced market. The CPQ provider helps companies automate their sales order process with a dynamic pricing, and an easy-to-use workflow approval process, accessible anywhere, on any device. Engineering teams were adding new features to their products every three months and handling custom implementations for clients across US, Europe & APAC.

"Imaginea worked with the company hand in hand as they focused heavily on product development to support sales-piling on new features as fast as possible"

The Game Changing Feature: Imaginea fully managed the biggest new addition to the CPQ product - a Proposal Editor. The feature allowed sales reps to port their quotes into a document format with a WYSIWYG editor and to drag and drop in text paragraphs to quickly assemble a professional-looking proposal, while enabling reuse of content. This feature was a significant selling point for the product and was built in less than 9 months, beating the customer's expectation of a year. The feature launch preceded what was the turning point in company's growth as they took on several big-name customers.

Better Configuration Options for The Product Users: Prior product versions included the ability to configure 100-200 line item products, but customers requested the ability to configure 500 lines or more. We achieved this using XML parsing technologies. We built complex configurators and proposals and implemented E2E project (configurators, commerce, proposals, salesforce.com integration)

The Unplugged Version: Close on the heels of this product enhancement, we create the much-requested "unplugged" version, requiring significant intelligence to manage the synchronization of information, data, and rules. Imaginea was the core product development team for this feature. It has been successfully implemented by several major customers of the company, including Symantec and a division of British Gas, which has over 1,000 sales reps using it.

Native Reporting Module: Another significant addition was the "native" reporting module. Around 2010 customers had been exporting data to other reporting systems, but we developed a Java-based solution that pulls the information from the database. We used JQuery and JFreeChart to a richer UI and drag and drop reports.

Added Version Control, E-sign Capability to Product: The CPQ product had no way to version control the changes or to identify when two people work on the same item simultaneously. A Firefox add-on called Composure was developed to achieve this. Solutions to creating documents that can be e-signed via multiple systems like Salesforce (docusgin), were created.

Redefined the UX/UI: We put an emphasis on usability through major enhancements to the UX/UI. Leveraging JQuery and GXT we were able to consolidate multiple functions and steps on a single page, making upgrades to the drag and drop capabilities and the WYSIWYG features.

The Infrastructure Upgrade and Improved Performance: On the infrastructure side, we introduced support for SOAP calls and integrated with automated Web service. Our teams brought in AJAX in multiple modules and saw significant performance improvements throughout the projects. We introduced Spring and Hibernate and rewrote some product features to improve performance.
