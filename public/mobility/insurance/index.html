Insurance companies need applications for everything from virtual information access to distribution and analytics. But at the heart of it all, the technology revolves around the customer. When the customer seeks information, requests a quote, buys a policy, needs after-sales service or wants a claims management platform, there's a vast layer of technologies that go into making it possible.

With smartphones becoming all-pervasive, insurance applications take a hugely rewarding step towards bridging the gap between the services they offer and a great user experience.

Enterprise Mobility Management

The mobility landscape is moving at warp speed. EMM vendors are adding new features every three months. Simple device management solutions are morphing into enterprise mobility suites. Capabilities are moving beyond device management - to content management, application provisioning and audit, and policy and service management.

As the race to dominate a market - expected to hit $175 billion in 2017 - heats up, vendors and their engineering teams are under intense pressure and face multiple challenges:

Scaling - solutions must work across very large number of corporate mobile users

Platform Diversity - multiple devices and interfaces, in a BYOD world - and new ones from Amazon, Microsoft, others

Quality Assurance - design, automation and execution of testing across multiple environments
